TAP lists from Evercore ISI

What you'll want to know

Have you explored Evercore ISI's TAP lists (short for Tactical, Action, Position) that offer a shorter-term lens on the market? These lists highlight stocks that Evercore analysts expect to outperform or underperform over the coming weeks or months.

Rather than long-term "buy" ratings, the focus is on near-term catalysts, such as upcoming earnings reports, management changes or shifts within a sector. A company might be added to the Tactical Outperform list ahead of an earnings announcement if Evercore believes expectations are too low—and removed once that catalyst passes or the story plays out.

Other stocks may appear on the Tactical Underperform list when near-term risks are expected to weigh on results. The TAP list is particularly useful during volatile markets or earnings season, when short-term catalysts tend to drive price moves.

Quadruple witching

Beware the witches! On four Fridays each year, stock and options markets fall under the spell of quadruple witching when several U.S. equity derivatives all expire at the same time. This can create wild swings in prices, especially in the last hour of the trading day.

Witching affects U.S. and other global markets, including Canada. Find out more about quadruple witching and learn how to prepare, so you don't get spooked. By the way, next witching day is Friday, March 20, 2026.

From stocks to tokens

Remember physical share certificates? That was the old-school way of owning shares, before everything went digital. Now another change is getting started with U.S. equities—tokenization. This is about taking shares that trade on a stock exchange and converting them to tokens that trade on a blockchain.

Why tokenize?

Tokenization can offer fractional ownership, 24/7 trading and instant settlement. What's more, it can bring new investors into capital markets. In many developing markets (and even some developed markets), it may be difficult for investors to set up brokerage accounts or exchange funds into U.S. dollars. Tokenization makes trading stocks like trading crypto, where investors set up a digital wallet and get immediate access to markets. The trend towards the tokenization of U.S. equities is likely to affect Canada much less than other global markets, since Canadians already have highly integrated access to major U.S. public equity markets (the vast majority of the world does not).

Where are we today?

The industry is at an early stage with tokenization. Companies are preparing to offer tokenized equities to U.S. retail investors, following guidance from the SEC in January 2026 that tokenized securities fall under existing federal laws. The Depository Trust and Clearing Company, which clears almost all U.S. stock trades, has launched a three-year tokenization initiative. Nasdaq and NYSE are also exploring ways to trade tokenized securities. And in the crypto world, there's a big question about whether stocks will be tokenized on private blockchains built by financial institutions or on public blockchains like Ethereum and Solana. More than a token development, it seems.
